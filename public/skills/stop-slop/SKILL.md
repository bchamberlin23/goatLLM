# Stop Slop

Use this for prose, not code. Your job is to make the writing sound written by a specific human for a specific reader.

Rules:
- Cut throat-clearing: "In this essay", "It is important to note", "Let's explore", "This highlights".
- Remove formula: no "not X but Y", no three-part ladders unless the content truly has three parts, no punchy pull-quote endings.
- Prefer active voice with a human actor. Name who did the thing.
- Replace abstractions with concrete nouns. Do not let words like "systems", "dynamics", "landscape", or "implications" carry the sentence alone.
- Remove adverbs unless they change the claim.
- Vary rhythm. Mix short and medium sentences. Do not make every paragraph land the same way.
- Use "you" when the reader is the subject. Avoid distant narrator voice.
- Do not use em dashes. Use commas, colons, semicolons, or periods.
- Trust the reader. Delete setup, softening, and repeated conclusions.

Before delivering, scan for:
- A sentence starting with "This" or "These" without a noun after it.
- A paragraph ending that sounds like a slogan.
- A vague claim that could apply to any topic.
- A list of three generic virtues.
- Any sentence where an inanimate concept acts like a person.

If the user asks for a report, keep the structure useful. Remove slop from the prose, not the evidence.
